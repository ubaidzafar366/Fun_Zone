  • Park Equipment Rentals:

Consider starting a business that offers equipment rentals and services to park visitors. This can include renting out various types of equipment such as:


Picnic Supplies:

Provide picnic baskets with snacks, sandwiches, and beverages.

Bicycles:

Rent out bicycles for individuals and families to explore the park's trails.

Outdoor Games:

Offer games like frisbee, volleyball, or badminton for group activities.

  • Guided Tours:

Guided Tours:

If you're knowledgeable about the park and its wildlife, consider offering guided tours. These could include nature walks, birdwatching tours, or even historical tours if the park has a significant historical aspect.

  • Services:

Food and Beverage Services:

Set up a mobile food and beverage kiosk or food truck near popular areas of the park. Offer snacks, ice cream, beverages, and even healthier options like salads and smoothies.

Pet Services:

If the park allows pets, consider offering pet-related services such as pet sitting, dog walking, or pet-friendly equipment rentals like pet strollers or bicycle trailers.

Photography Services:

If you have photography skills, consider offering photography sessions within the park. You can take family portraits, engagement photos, or capture the natural beauty of the park for visitors.

Park Cleanup Services:

Offer park cleanup and maintenance services to keep the park clean and beautiful. This can be especially lucrative if the park hosts events or has a high volume of visitors.
